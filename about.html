Our story

Bowling is back on the Westbank.

Six decades of strikes, spares and Saturday nights — and we're just getting warmed up.

Est. 1960s · Reopened 2016

A Westbank institution, renovated for a new generation.

The name Westside Lanes is synonymous with great bowling on the Westbank of New Orleans. Originally founded in the 1960s, Westside was the first bowling alley on this side of the river, and soon became one of many beloved venues in the area.

Changing fortunes over the years resulted in the closure of those other establishments — including our historic venue in 2002. For nearly fifteen years, area bowlers had to cross the river to roll a frame. That changed in 2016, after a major renovation that began in 2014, when Westside officially reopened as the only bowling alley on the Westbank.

As longtime members of this community for over fifty years, we take pride in our local heritage — but we also blend the past with the future by offering the premier bowling experience in greater New Orleans.

The timeline

Six decades, one Westbank tradition.

1960s

First on the Westbank

Westside Lanes opens, bringing the first dedicated bowling alley to this side of the river.

2002

Doors close

After decades of league nights and family tournaments, the original building closes — leaving a gap on the Westbank.

2014

Rebuild begins

Renovation kicks off. Twenty new lanes, a full bar, two kitchens, and a fresh bistreaux all planned under one roof.

2016

Grand reopening

Westside Lanes reopens — the Westbank's only bowling alley, and the newest entertainment venue in Jefferson Parish.

Today

NOLA favorite

Weekend DJs, packed leagues, birthday parties, weddings, and fundraisers — Westside is where the Westbank gathers.
